# Atelier deck — sourced comparables fact sheet

Date: 2026-07-17 · Method: deep-research harness (5 search angles, 22 sources fetched,
104 claims extracted, 25 adversarially verified by 3-vote panels → 24 confirmed, 1
refuted). Public sources only. **Confidence labels:** `verified` = confirmed 3-0 and
corroborated across independent outlets; `vendor-claimed` = confirmed as accurately
quoted but the number originates from the vendor/investor with no independent audit —
usable in the deck ONLY with explicit attribution ("vendor X reports…").

## (a) Demo-experience-platform category — the market exists and is consolidating

- **Consensus raised $110M from Sumeru Equity Partners** — its largest round, to scale
  enterprise presales. Business Wire, 2023-03-08, `businesswire.com/news/home/20230308005208`.
  Confidence: **verified** (independent coverage: PYMNTS, Utah Business; deal counsel
  confirmed). Nuance: single-PE growth investment, not a multi-investor VC round.
- **Consensus revenue +60% in 2022** — stated by Sumeru MD Jason Babcoke in the same
  release. Confidence: **vendor/investor-claimed** (attribute to the investor quote).
- **Consensus is consolidating the category in 2026**: definitive agreement to acquire
  Peel announced 2026-04-22 (PR Newswire `302749813`); acquisition of Saleo + closing of
  Peel announced 2026-06-09 (PR Newswire `302795244`, corroborated by Morningstar/Yahoo
  syndication). No financial terms disclosed. Confidence: **verified**.
- **Consensus adoption/impact boilerplate** — "trusted by 60% of the world's largest
  software companies (Oracle, Atlassian, Salesforce, SAP)"; "shortens sales cycles by
  29% up to 68%, improves close rates by up to 44%". Same three releases. Confidence:
  **vendor-claimed** (no methodology; undefined denominator). The more specific "15 of
  the 30 largest software companies" roster was **REFUTED (1-2) — do not use**.
- **Reprise TEI metrics** — Forrester Consulting Total Economic Impact study
  *commissioned by Reprise* (composite of 4 customers): 323% ROI over 3 years ($1.01M
  NPV), 60% pipeline-conversion increase, 20% less sales-engineering involvement, 50%
  marketing-productivity gain, 1-week faster sales onboarding. PR Newswire syndication
  `301491562`, 2022-02-28 (original reprise.com URL now 404 — cite the syndication).
  Confidence: **vendor-commissioned** (TEI = paid consulting; date it as Feb 2022).
- **Gaps:** no Walnut or Demostack funding/outcome claims survived verification; treat
  those names as category members only, without numbers.

**Deck implication:** the category slide can say, verified: a funded ($110M) and
consolidating (Peel + Saleo, 2026) product category exists for demo experiences —
proving buyers pay for experience-led selling. All conversion metrics must carry
"vendor-reported" attribution or be omitted.

## (b) Vendor pre-sales serious games — SAP institutionalized the format

- **SAP S/4HANA Movement virtual board game** (launched Nov 2020, COVID-era replacement
  for in-person innovation workshops): facilitator-led (presales/value advisor/partner)
  with customer stakeholders; three stages (educate → identify value drivers → define
  outcomes); playable per line of business; positioned "as early as possible, during
  the Discovery phase of the sales/buying cycle"; tangible outputs = customer S/4HANA
  Roadmap + Value Map/business case; audience includes customers, partners,
  universities AND SAP internal enablement (Academy, Presales, Sales, Value Advisory).
  Evolved from a 2018 physical discovery-cards game. Sources: SAP News Nov 2020 (now
  404 — cite Wayback snapshot `web.archive.org/web/20260512025004/...`), SAP Community
  post ID 13362815, SAP webinar PDF 2020-04-29. Confidence: **verified** (format &
  positioning; all SAP-authored). "Thousands have played" is vendor-reported.
- **SAP BTP Diamond Game**: physical board game by SAP UKI BTP team (designers Raphael
  Walter, Pierre-Yves Tohme); small customer teams role-play one company solving real
  business issues with BTP "service cards"; live practice — SAP runs "Diamond Game
  Experience Day" events and Gold Partner Bluestonex sells facilitated workshops for
  use-case discovery. SAP UK News 2023-10-26; bluestonex.com (fetched 2026-07-17).
  Confidence: **verified** (existence/format/commercial practice).
- **SAP simulation games on live systems**: ERPsim (created by HEC Montréal's ERPsim
  Lab — NOT by SAP; SAP runs sessions) and "SAP S/4HANA Simulation by Baton
  Simulations" — teams of 10–25 with business roles run an end-to-end cycle on a live
  system; published scenarios "Water Distribution" (2–3h, "impactful with executives")
  and "Muesli Manufacturing" (3.5h–1 day). SAP UK News 2023-10-26 + SAP webinar PDF
  2020-04-29 + erpsim.hec.ca. Confidence: **verified** (format/duration).
- **SAP publishes NO quantitative outcomes for any of these games.** Confidence:
  verified absence (direct fetches).

**Deck implication:** SAP supports a "major vendors institutionalize facilitated
serious games as pre-sales instruments" narrative — format validation for Boardroom
Quest — never an ROI claim.

## (c) AI enablement of a commercial/enterprise workforce — one strong dated case

- **Moderna × OpenAI (ChatGPT Enterprise), April 2024**: within two months of adoption
  (~3,000 employees) — 750 custom GPTs across the company; 40% of weekly active users
  created their own GPTs; average 120 conversations/user/week. Source: OpenAI customer
  story `openai.com/index/moderna` (Apr 2024), independently reported with identical
  figures by Constellation Research 2024-04-24. Confidence: **vendor-claimed**
  (numbers supplied by Moderna via OpenAI; the 120/week figure is unusually high —
  flag as vendor-reported). Present as "OpenAI customer story, April 2024".
- **Gaps:** no Klarna, Accenture, or Microsoft sales-copilot claims survived
  adversarial verification. Do not cite them.

**Deck implication:** the enablement slide leans on Moderna (attributed) + the
structural argument (employees building their own AI tools at scale within weeks),
not on a wall of numbers.

## (d) BDR/SDR + AI productivity & deal-size benchmarks — EMPTY after verification

**No named analyst or vendor BDR/SDR AI-productivity or deal-size benchmark survived
adversarial verification.** Candidate sources examined included Gartner's 2025-11-18
press release (AI agents to outnumber sellers 10× by 2028, yet <40% of sellers
reporting productivity gains) and McKinsey's generative-AI B2B-sales essay — none
yielded a defensible per-rep productivity or deal-size number.

**Deck implication (binding):** the KPI/plan sections must NOT quote external BDR
productivity or deal-size benchmarks. Instead: define OUR OWN measurable KPIs
(time-to-first-experience, % autonomous creation, influenced pipeline) and let the
milestones create the evidence. This is also the stronger executive story: we measure
ourselves rather than borrowing numbers.
